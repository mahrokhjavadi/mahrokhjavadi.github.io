About Me

I am an Optimization Researcher and Algorithm Engineer with a Ph.D. (Summa Cum Laude) in Computational Intelligence. My expertise lies in evolutionary computation, multi-objective optimization, and algorithmic decision systems bridging academic research and industrial deployment.

My doctoral research focused on evolutionary and metaheuristic optimization for multi-objective and multimodal problems in complex, high-dimensional search spaces. I investigated strategies for maintaining population diversity, mitigating premature convergence, and improving exploration–exploitation balance in evolutionary algorithms.

Algorithmic performance was evaluated on established benchmark suites including ZDT, DTLZ, and CEC test problems. Experimental methodology included multiple independent runs, statistical performance comparison, and non-parametric hypothesis testing using the Mann–Whitney U test to ensure rigorous and reproducible evaluation.

In industry, I worked as a Senior Optimization & Algorithm Engineer at KLA (semiconductor metrology), where I developed and optimized high-precision algorithmic systems for data-intensive measurement workflows. My contributions improved computational efficiency and measurement robustness and contributed to a U.S. patent application in optimization-assisted industrial metrology.

Alongside research and industry work, I design end-to-end optimization-driven decision systems integrating machine learning, mathematical programming (MILP), and scalable data engineering. I am particularly interested in roles where methodological rigor, algorithm design, and real-world impact intersect.
