About Me

I am a Development and Research Engineer with a Ph.D. in Computational Intelligence and 5+ years of experience applying optimization, data engineering, and machine learning to real-world industrial systems.

My doctoral research focused on metaheuristic and multi-objective optimization, with emphasis on avoiding local optima and improving solution diversity in complex search spaces. This work resulted in peer-reviewed publications and established a rigorous foundation in algorithm design, problem formulation, and evaluation.

In industry, I worked as an Algorithm Developer at KLA in semiconductor metrology, developing and optimizing algorithms for high-precision, data-intensive workflows. My contributions improved runtime efficiency and measurement performance and contributed to a U.S. patent application in semiconductor metrology optimization.

Alongside industrial work, I built end-to-end projects spanning IoT energy data pipelines, analytics, ML-based forecasting, and MILP optimization for energy systems. I enjoy roles where algorithms, data, and engineering combine to create robust, scalable solutions with measurable impact.
